COURSE DESCRIPTION

Barburys cross country course travels over rolling downland with much of it utilising old turf gallops. The well drained chalkland promises good going and retains plenty of spring and turf cover even in a dry summer.

There are changes in 2013 for the Novice, CIC2* and CIC3* cross country courses. Rolling downland with much of the course utilising old turf gallops promising good going on well drained chalkland soil that will retain plenty of spring and turf cover even in a dry summer. The fences are mostly permanent but some portables. The Willis Brothers have built an exciting, challenging and fair course. The hills will test stamina. The Novice course will be an up -to-height, flowing course which might not suit the first time Novice competitor.

The Cross Country course was newly built for the first event in July 2005. Designed by International Course Designer, Captain Mark Phillips, the fences are mostly permanent with some portables. and XC on Sunday. Please be prepared to do your dressage on any of the days.

CLASSES AND FEES

The INu21 is the Junior Final Trial and open to invited combinations only.

The IN class is ONLY for horses competing in the ROR Championship entries and is open to any grade of horse and will run as an IN on the Friday - entries will be accepted on a first come first accepted basis. The top 10 will be invited to SJ again for the top money on Sunday. Please register with the ROR email: darbuthnot@ror.org.uk. Some Novice dressage will take place on Thursday afternoon - priority will be given to those who are available to do this.

CIC2* dressage on Thursday and Friday and Show Jump and XC on Saturday. CIC3* will do dressage on Thursday and Friday, SJ on Saturday and XC on Sunday. Please be prepared to do your dressage on any of the days.
